Art and Medicine

The O’Donnell Institute partners with UT Southwestern Medical School and the Dallas Museum of Art to train physicians and medical students in the art of observation.

Art of Examination: Art and Medical Diagnosis

A growing number of medical schools are incorporating the arts into their curricula to develop students’ skills in observation and diagnosis.

The O’Donnell Institute partners with The University of Texas Southwestern Medical School and the Dallas Museum of Art to engage students in observing, analyzing, and communicating about works of art to develop their diagnostic skills.

Bonnie Pitman, Distinguished Scholar in Residence at the O’Donnell Institute, Dr. Heather Wickless at UT Southwestern Medical School, and Amanda Blake at the DMA have designed an innovative course using the museum’s collections to explore ways of looking at works of art through close examination, description, empathy, and insights into the human condition.

National Forum on Art Museums and Medical Schools

In June 2016, 120 invited guests from art museums and medical schools throughout the country will gather at the Museum of Modern Art in New York for a National Forum on Art Museums and Medical Schools.

The Art of Examination: Art Museums with Medical Schools Partnerships Forum is convened by Bonnie Pitman, Distinguished Scholar in Residence at the O’Donnell Institute and Wendy Woon, The Edward John Noble Foundation Deputy Director for Education at MoMA.

A principle goal of the Forum is to foster collaborations between museums and medical schools to develop programs that strengthen diagnostic skills through the art of observation, description, interpretation, empathy, and communication.
