About Our Research

The O’Donnell Institute is dedicated to fostering innovative research in art history, drawing on the strengths of public and private collections and exploring in particular the intersections between the visual arts and the sciences and technology.

Scholars at the O’Donnell Institute study and teach a broad range of subjects, from Islamic manuscripts to Renaissance maps to contemporary painting.

Our work centers on artworks and research materials held in area collections and institutions, and our research programs aim to foster scholarly exchange and creativity.

Drawing on the strengths of The University of Texas at Dallas, the O’Donnell Institute is also distinguished by its dedication to becoming a center for the study of Conservation Science and to exploring new intersections between the visual arts and the sciences and technology.
