Graduate Program

A new Master’s program at the O’Donnell Institute will introduce students to a world history of art through close engagement with artworks held in Dallas-Fort Worth collections.

Edith O’Donnell’s vision for the institute she founded includes a new Master’s program in art history, and her endowment provides graduate fellowships for young scholars of outstanding potential.

In Fall 2017, pending University approval, the Edith O’Donnell Institute of Art History will launch a Master’s program that will be a collaboration between the O’Donnell Institute, The University of Texas at Dallas, and the Dallas Museum of Art.

The planned Master’s Program in Art History at the O’Donnell Institute will train students who wish to pursue careers in research, education, museums, and conservation, and will foster in particular the exploration of interdisciplinary approaches to art history.

Our proposed curriculum is designed to introduce students to a world history of art through close engagement with artworks held in Dallas-Fort Worth collections, developing students’ knowledge of a broad range of material across geography, chronology, and medium and building strong foundations in historiography, theory, and professional practices.

The planned Master’s Program at the O’Donnell Institute will be distinguished by

  • a close partnership with the Dallas Museum of Art, providing unique access to the Museum’s collections and research resources
  • the broad range of specialties of Institute-affiliated scholars, and the O’Donnell Institute’s particular strength in interdisciplinary connections between the visual arts and the sciences
  • a practicum element in which students design and carry out an original project in the form of a scholarly essay, an exhibition, a collaboration with a practicing artist, a conservation project, or archival research
  • and the opportunity to participate in the scholarly life of a center for advanced research
